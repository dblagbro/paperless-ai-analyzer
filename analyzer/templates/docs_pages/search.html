Search & Analysis

Semantic full-text search with AI-generated summaries, risk scoring, and anomaly filtering — scoped to the current project.

Works immediately after restart. Search queries the Chroma vector store directly — all embedded documents are searchable the moment the container starts. There is no warm-up period and results do not depend on which documents happened to be processed in the current session.

How search works

Every search goes directly to the Chroma vector store, which holds all embedded documents with their full AI-generated metadata. Two complementary approaches are used together:

  • Semantic search — your query is embedded (Cohere) and compared against document vectors. Finds documents by meaning — a search for "payment discrepancy" will surface documents about "balance mismatch" or "missing transaction" even if those exact words don't appear in your query.
  • Exact substring match — a secondary pass searches document title, brief summary, full summary, and anomaly fields for exact matches. This ensures a search for a document ID like 1398 or a specific title keyword always returns the correct result.

Performing a search

  1. Switch to the project you want to search (header dropdown).
  2. Open the Search & Analysis tab.
  3. Type your query in the search box.
  4. Optionally apply filters (see below).
  5. Press Enter or click Search.

Filters

  • Anomalies only — check to see only documents that have at least one anomaly tag.
  • Risk score range — set min/max to filter by forensic risk score (0–100).

Search tips

  • Use natural language: "bank statements with overdrafts" works better than "overdraft".
  • Include entity names: "documents involving John Smith", "invoices from Acme Corp".
  • Include dates or periods: "statements from Q1 2025", "filings after March 15".
  • Combine terms: "court orders regarding receiver payments 2024".
  • For anomaly investigation: search "balance discrepancy" and enable Anomalies only.

Recent Analyses section

Below the search area, the Recent Analyses list shows the 50 most recently processed documents in the current project. This list auto-refreshes every 10 seconds but pauses while you have the Search tab active to prevent results jumping while you read.
